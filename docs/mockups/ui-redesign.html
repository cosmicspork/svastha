Svastha UI redesign — review mockup
Design review · July 2026 · from field notes on the current build

Eight decisions before we build

Everything below keeps the identity you already have — porcelain, fern, Young Serif — and spends its energy on the things your notes flagged: the bottom bar, mindfulness, forms, first-run, keys, linking, theme, and the unlock screen. Each section says what gets locked in if you approve it. The demos are live: tap the + button, switch the mood tabs, flip the theme.

DECISION 01

The bloom replaces the bar

The current bar spends a permanent 56 px strip on six labels. It goes away entirely; the timeline runs to the bottom edge. In its place: one fern + in the corner that fans seven petals through the top-left quarter — never over the thumb.

Petals sit on one quarter-arc with clear air between them, so each reads as its own button. Order is frequency: top petal is your most-logged kind, sweeping down-left to the least. The default order below is a guess (vitals first — you log BP daily); after a few weeks the app reorders from actual counts, and only reorders when the change is decisive, so petals don't jitter day to day.

One-handed on the other side? A setting mirrors the whole gesture to the bottom-left (see Decision 07).

Locks in

  • Bottom bar deleted; timeline gains the strip + safe area
  • 7 petals: glyph + mono label, one 90° arc, clear air between petals
  • Frequency order, top → bottom-left, adaptive with hysteresis
  • Scrim dims the page while open; + rotates to ×
  • Mirrored layout behind a setting

Trade-off, named: logging becomes two taps instead of one. The favorites row inside each form still gives one-tap re-logging of your staples. If it stings, a long-press on + could jump straight to the top petal — say the word and it's in scope.

DECISION 02

Mindfulness: mood & gratitude

A seventh category, built to correlate. Mood is a five-step scale drawn as a filling moon — wordless enough to answer honestly, ordinal so Patterns can chart it against food, meds, sleep and movement like any other series. Gratitude reuses the chip entry you already know from Food: short entries, not essays.

The category gets its own hue — orchid — chosen for the one open gap in the existing category wheel (between med-violet and symptom-plum), and the ✿ glyph. Both work in light and dark.

Locks in

  • New category mind: glyph ✿, token --cat-mind (#8a5f96 / #bd93c7)
  • Mood = 1–5 ordinal + optional short note; charts in Patterns
  • Gratitude = chip entries, 1–3 encouraged, no minimum
  • One form, segmented Mood | Gratitude (same pattern as Vitals' picker)

Open: petal label — the fan says Mind below; Mood is friendlier but hides gratitude. Also: should a day's mood be once-a-day (edit replaces) or log-many like everything else? Mockup assumes log-many, Patterns takes the day's median.

DECISION 03

Forms: actions live in one bar

The stray Cancel you saw on iOS is the current shell's floating header button colliding with the dynamic-island safe area. The fix is structural, not a patch: every action moves into a sticky bar pinned above the home indicator — Cancel quiet on the left, Save loud on the right, "Save & another" tonal in between. Nothing actionable floats near the notch again.

The header simplifies to a category dot + title. Field labels become one shared .field class — today six forms each re-declare the same label CSS. Favorites keep their row under the title; ⚡ still logs a staple in one tap.

Locks in

  • Sticky .action-bar: Cancel / Save & another / Save, safe-area padded
  • Header = kind dot + title, nothing tappable in the top corners
  • Shared .field label style replaces six local copies
  • "When: now / Earlier" row unchanged — it works

DECISION 04

First run: earn the home screen

After the vault exists and you land on the timeline for the first time, one sheet makes the case for installing — Svastha is local-first, so the home-screen app is the product. Platform-specific steps (iOS share sheet vs. Android install), then it stays gone: dismissal writes a flag to the local DB alongside your other prefs, and the sheet never shows when already running standalone.

Locks in

  • One-time install sheet on first unlocked visit
  • Steps switch on platform; skipped entirely in standalone mode
  • Dismissed = persisted pref, not a cookie; survives reloads
  • Re-reachable later from Settings → About, never re-nags

DECISION 05

Keys you can actually copy

You couldn't select the words on iOS because the grid is deliberately user-select: none — an anti-screenshot instinct that ended up just being friction. The paper advice stays, but an explicit Copy phrase button respects that a password manager is a legitimate home for a seed. The honest trade-off is stated right under the button, and the clipboard is cleared after sixty seconds.

Locks in

  • Copy button on the onboarding words step and the Settings reveal
  • Clipboard auto-clears after 60 s (when the page is still open)
  • Grid stays non-selectable — the button is the sanctioned path
  • Paper-first copy tone kept, minus the scolding

DECISION 06

Linking by QR, both directions

Two QR flows, one vocabulary. Device → device: Settings grows a Devices section; “Link a new device” shows a QR carrying the relay address plus a one-time invite. The new device gets a third onboarding path — Link this device — scans, connects, pulls. Relay → device: the relay's landing page stops being blank; it states its zero-knowledge role and shows a QR that hands the relay address to the app, replacing URL-typing on a phone keyboard.

Locks in

  • Settings → Devices: list of linked devices, revoke, “Link a new device”
  • Onboarding gains a third path: Create / Restore / Link
  • QR carries relay URL + one-time invite token, 10-minute expiry
  • Relay serves a self-describing landing page with its QR
  • Reuses the existing uqr renderer from Share — no new dependency

Trust-contract question, flagged early: can the QR spare the new device from typing the seed phrase? Auto-provisioning keys over a QR-brokered channel touches ARCHITECTURE.md. The mockup assumes the cautious version — QR moves address + invite, the seed is still entered on the new device — and the ambitious version is a separate design round.

DECISION 07

Appearance that does something

The button that “didn't seem to do anything” was never a theme control — it picks the accent hue for the timeline spine, and nothing on the settings screen said so. Appearance becomes three honest controls: Theme (light / dark / system — today the app only follows the OS), the hue swatches renamed Timeline accent with a caption, and Add button for the left-hand bloom.

The segmented control in this frame is wired to this whole page — set it to Dark and watch the palette you already approved hold up.

Locks in

  • Theme pref: light / dark / system, default system, persisted
  • Hue swatches renamed “Timeline accent”, with explanatory caption
  • “Add button: right / left” lives here too
  • Both existing palettes kept exactly as-is — the green stays

DECISION 08

Unlock is the front door

You see this screen more than any other — every app open — and today it's the barest one in the app: a top-left heading, a field, and a browser window.confirm() on the forgot path. It becomes the one screen where the brand gets to breathe: centered wordmark over a vault seal — a ring of 24 ticks, one per seed word — that wakes to fern while you type.

Below the wordmark, the identity fingerprint says whose vault this device holds before anything is decrypted — the honest self-custody detail, pulled from what Settings already shows. The mechanics you have are kept exactly: autofocus, password-manager autofill, inline Show, Enter submits. The forgot path still wipes and restores from seed, but the confirm becomes an in-app sheet — same component as first-run — instead of a browser dialog.

Locks in

  • Centered composition: seal + wordmark + fingerprint, field in the upper half (clears the iOS keyboard)
  • Vault seal: 24 ticks = 24 words; border-colored at rest, fern while typing, madder on error
  • Identity fingerprint shown pre-unlock, in the owner's timeline accent
  • Autofocus, autocomplete="current-password", inline Show — all kept
  • Forgot-path confirm becomes a sheet; the wipe warning copy stays verbatim

Open: passkey unlock (Face ID / fingerprint via WebAuthn) so trusted devices skip typing entirely. Real and worth doing, but it touches key derivation — same treatment as the QR seed handoff: a separate trust-contract round.
